So how does it work?

The letters are inserted into the box via the flap on the top part. A VCNL4010 proximity sensor detects when the flap has been opened and mail thrown in the postbox. It then immediately notifies you that you’ve got new mail. No matter how many letters the postman wants to deliver to you, SmartBox will not fill up your electronic mailbox with spam as many times as the postman has opened the door. It only sends you one notification every time you received a new delivery of letters.

And what’s behind the pretty cover? We have made it convenient for you to access your mail. There is no need to always take an extra key with you so that you could open your mailbox to find nothing useful in it. SmartBox can be opened with your mobile phone easily without the need to remember which key opens your letterbox.

SmartBox looks like a traditional postbox so that it looks good on your front door, or mounted to your fence. It is very easy to set up your own device as it automatically connects to your Wifi network. Since SmartBox is solar powered, there is no need to find a plug to make it work. It is very easy to fit it on the wall; all the necessary screws and nuts are provided in the box. The device is designed to use as little energy as possible. A little light is provided within the box, but it only lights up when you really need it in the dark to save power. This is achieved by the ambient light measuring functionality of the VCNL4010 sensor.

Technical Details

  • Two VCNL4010 sensors are utilised:
    • one for ambient light sensing
    • one for proximity measurement
  • Sensor limitations: max. 20 cm distance and 16000 lux
  • I2C communication with the processing unit
  • Functions implemented using the ESP8266 board, interfaced through the NodeMCU board
  • Size: H: 32 cm x W: 22 cm x D: 8 cm
  • Weight: 1.6 kg
  • Door opens with Tower pro SG92R servo motor
  • Lamp: Power saving LED panel
  • Solar charger unit

The Future

Developing a smart postbox gives rise to lots of new opportunities with traditional post services. We are planning to implement several new features in the future editions of the device. By measuring the weight of the letters, we could give more information about the returned content. Little slips could be marked as spam for example. The next level would be to scan the letters for name and address, and classify the unwanted adverts as spam. The user would have online access to the contents of the box. The implementation of advanced locking/unlocking systems could later lead to a solution to home-deliveries, even with the online monitoring of the couriers.
